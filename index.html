Master the Art of Creating a Textured Mug Cozy

Your Progress

0 of 7 steps completed

Project Details

Skill Level: Beginner

Construction: A stretchy ribbed rectangle worked flat in rows; button loop forms closure

Finished Size: ~2.5–3 in (6–7.5 cm) tall × 9–11 in (23–28 cm) long, adjustable to mug

Materials Needed

Yarn: Worsted weight yarn (approx. 50-75 yards)

Hook: Size H/8 (5.0mm) crochet hook

Notions: Yarn needle, scissors, 1 button

Gauge: ~8–9 hdc sts × 6–7 rows ≈ 2 in × 2 in in BLO (flexible)

🧶 Visualizing Stitch Anatomy (Quick Guide)

🔍
Top of each stitch shows a "V". BLO means insert hook under the back bar of the V (farther from you). Edges stay straight when you always work the first and last stitch—count them each row.
1

Foundation Chain

Start your mug cozy by creating the foundation chain that determines the height.

Instructions: Chain 9 (for ~2.5 in height). For taller cozy, chain 11.
Keep your foundation chain loose and even. This will make it easier to work into for your first row!
2

First Row

Work your first row of half double crochet stitches across the foundation chain.

Instructions: Half double crochet (hdc) in 2nd chain from hook and in each chain across. (8 or 10 stitches total)
Remember to skip the first chain (closest to your hook) and start in the second chain. Count your stitches to ensure you have the right number!
3

Second Row (BLO Pattern)

Begin the textured pattern by working in back loops only.

Instructions: Chain 1, turn. Half double crochet in back loop only (BLO) across. (maintain 8 or 10 stitches)
Working in back loops only creates the ribbed texture! Insert your hook under only the back bar of each "V" at the top of the stitches.
4

Repeat Rows

Continue the ribbed pattern until your cozy is long enough to wrap around your mug.

Instructions: Repeat Row 2 (ch 1, turn, hdc in BLO across) until length wraps the mug with slight tension (about 9–11 inches).
Test fit your cozy around your mug as you go! You want it snug but not too tight. The ribbed texture will provide stretch.
5

Button Loop

Create the button loop closure for your mug cozy.

Instructions: At end of last row, chain 8 (or to fit button), slip stitch to last stitch to form a loop. Fasten off, leaving 8–10 inch tail.
Test your button through the loop before fastening off. Adjust the chain count if needed - you want it snug but not too tight!
6

Finishing Touches

Weave in ends and attach the button to complete your cozy.

Instructions: Using yarn needle, weave ends neatly. Sew button centered on opposite short edge. Try on mug and adjust.
Position the button so it aligns perfectly with your loop when the cozy is wrapped around your mug. This ensures a secure fit!
7

Optional Edging

Add a polished finish with an optional single crochet border.

Instructions: Work 1 round of single crochet around entire rectangle for a framed look (place 1 sc per row end; 3 sc in each corner).
This edging step gives your cozy a professional, finished appearance and helps prevent curling at the edges!

🔧 Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Edges are slanting: You may be skipping the first or last stitch. Count stitches each row (8 or 10).
  • Too tight: Loosen grip; try a 5.5 mm hook for easier motion.
  • Too loose/holes: Pull a little more yarn control; try a 4.5 mm hook.
  • Loop too small/large: Adjust chain count for the button loop to fit snugly.
  • Uneven height: Recount foundation chain; add/remove one chain to dial height.
